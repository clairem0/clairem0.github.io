Career Short Story

I started in management consulting at Parthenon. I wanted to challenge myself in a high-paced, competitive professional environment. But knowledge work didn't feel very real. So I went to a tomato farm in Maine, working with the leadership team at a commercial farm that supplied 20% of New England's tomatoes. I learned a ton, and Maine was lonely.

I moved to California to go to grad school and try out a whole new life. Coming from New England, I was amazed by the sunshine and you-can-be-anything Western mindset. From there, I took a role in general management at Big Heart Pet Brands, where I ran the $300m Milk-Bone business. That was great big company training, and I felt ready to build.

I joined Philz Coffee to grow the company from $20m to $100m in revenue, and work with some of the best leaders I've known. Retail makes great people leaders. And now I'm at Guild, where I went to learn tech and I lead a 30-person business technology and AI transformation team.

Enterprise AI Portfolio & Architecture
Portfolio strategy, spend management, and architecture decisions that keep the company flexible as the stack evolves.
Guild
▸

AI spend is consumption-based and the stack is unbundling. Portfolio strategy, spend management, and architecture all need to evolve together.

  • Open licensing: Robust AI productivity portfolio for 800+ users (ChatGPT, Claude Cowork, Codex, Claude Code, Replit, Cursor, etc). We want users consuming tokens.
  • Legacy spend harvesting: Aggressively reduce legacy vendor seats as users shift to agent interfaces. Savings reinvested into AI.
  • Consumption-based budgeting: New software spend approach with FP&A to treat AI spend like opex/headcount. Users own their investment decisions for accountability.
  • Architecture philosophy: Don't lock business logic to one vendor. Keep integrations portable and own the data.
